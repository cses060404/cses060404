Mobile Browsing - Analysis

Introduction

In the task, we were asked to use our mobile devices to browse for 24 hours instead of using

desktop or laptop. The purpose of this task is to help us having a better understanding of

how it is like to browser in a tiny screen for a long time. I was able to pay more attention

on the sites that I have always been browsing and I did have different feelings about those sites.

Here are some of the example questions to help us know how the experiment went.

Questions to consider:

  • Can you still see what you want to see?
  • Can you find what you're looking for?
  • Can you do what you want to do?
  • Any frustrations?
  • Anything work better on the small screen?

Findings

Can you still see what you want to see?

I can still pretty much see all the information that I wanna to see.

However, for the website that I use the most -- BYUI I-Learn, I cannot see

the couse links but only the assignments. Sometimes I just want to view a

specific couse and the grade of it, without the tab, it's harder to gain

access to it.

Can you find what you're looking for?

Can you do what you want to do?

Any frustrations?

Definitely! Since the screen is so small, I have to keep scrolling down when I

want to read a really long article. For example, I like to read news, and I found

it extremely difficult when I want to read stories. Another thing bother me the

most is that I cannot see the mouse and sometimes it is difficult to "touch" at the

right thing that I want to click on.

Anything work better on the small screen?

One adventage I like about the small screen is that my eyeballs don't need to look

around in order to find the button or whatever information that I need. I bet

that everyone had encounter this problem before when they want to find something from the

big srceen, they looked all over the screen and still cannot find the login button.

Another thing I like aobut the small screen is that it is so much simpler that they

hide most of the unnecessary imformations and ads I don't really need for my life.
